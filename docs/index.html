Have you ever heard of the BigMac Pizza Index ? Using the average price of a pizza in all the states, we could derive and Index centered on New York that quantifies this value across the country. If you click on the toggle button, you can compare it to the living cost index.

Slide to the right if you want to learn more about it.

Here is a visualization of the pizza index by state. How did we build this ? For every state, we averaged the prices of the pizzas we have. Then we compute a ratio by dividing it by the price average of New York state. This means New York is our reference and the index is centered around it. Note that the pizza index for New York is therefore 1.0. By looking at the graph, we can easily spot the states where pizzas are more expensive, such as California or Mississippi. Now we want to use this information and compare it to the living cost index of the states and try to see if the conclusions we can draw are mostly the same.

Unfortunately, after a deeper analysis, we found that the pizza index wasnâ€™t the best index to draw attention to the social and economical differences between the American states. While California may have a pizza index that accurately reflects its cost of living, the majority of Northern states demonstrate a pizza index that contradicts the actual cost of living in those areas. However, this can be due to the fact that we have very little data for those states. This means that the average is based on few values, which can lead to arbitrary conclusions.
By studying the correlation between the pizza index and the living cost of the different states, we find that they are slightly positively correlated, as the Pearson and Spearman correlation are 0.137 and 0.124. However, this result is not statistically significant as the p values are too high (0.352 and 0.398).
